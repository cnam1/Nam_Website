What You See

2018

Living in Chicago, I sometimes experience homesickness, which I've channeled through a unique medium: Hwatu cards. Hwatu is a deck of cards traditionally used for games like Go-Stop and is beloved in Korea, often played during family gatherings. By integrating these culturally significant game cards into the context of Chicago, I've aimed to express my deep thoughts about and longing for home. When positioning the cards, I drew significant inspiration from Kenneth Josephson's photography works. I was particularly intrigued by how he arranges images and objects within a composition to create a connection with the background. By matching the background image of Chicago with the drawings on the cards, I aimed to bridge the gap between two distinct cultures, creating a sense of connection.
